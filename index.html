Your 5 Personal Rules 📋
01
Work WITH Your Mom, Not Around Her 🏠
Don't try to eat differently from what she cooks — just adjust portions and add protein. Extra dal scoop, fewer rotis, more sabzi. Indian home food is already clean. You don't need "diet food."
02
Protect Your Sleep Like It's a Gym Session 😴
You're sleeping 5.5–6 hours. At 18, with 6-day training, that's actively fighting your fat loss. Poor sleep spikes hunger hormones and tanks recovery. Aim to be in bed by 10:30pm. This single change could speed results by 30%.
03
The Chai Habit Isn't the Enemy — The Biscuits Are ☕
Keep your evening chai. It's cultural, it's comforting, it's yours. Just replace what goes with it. Roasted chana, rice cakes, a boiled egg — same ritual, completely different outcome.
04
Use Your Bus Time as a Weapon 🚌
2 hours on a bus daily = perfect time to track your food, plan tomorrow's meals, or do a quick check-in. Most people waste this time. You can use it to stay ahead of the game.
05
Steps Before Gym Obsession 👟
8,000–10,000 steps daily will burn more total calories than an extra gym session for most people. Walk to the bus stop. Walk after college. It all counts and doesn't exhaust your recovery.

🌅
Start before college: Drink 500ml water before you leave the house — you'll forget once the day starts.
🚌
Bus bottle rule: Keep a 1-litre bottle on the bus. Both journeys = 2 litres done before dinner.
☕
Make chai count less: For every cup of chai, drink one glass of water — chai is a mild diuretic.
🌙
Night signal: If you're snacking at night, drink 300ml water first — 70% of late-night "hunger" is actually thirst.
